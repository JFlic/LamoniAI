Get more than a major — develop skills that will last a lifetime.

This is where it all begins. Where you start a journey of academic exploration, take chances, tap into your curiosity and discover the path that’s right for you. With 31 undergraduate programs,  eight pre-professional programs, seven master’s programs, and two doctoral programs at Graceland University, you’ll have plenty of room to explore — both in the classroom and with hands-on experiential learning. In fact, we believe that building strong leaders and changing lives happens through all our educational opportunities, be that academic or otherwise, and joining the Graceland community is about more than just earning a quality degree — it’s about discovering who you are, what you want to become and going after it.

Ranked as one of the top nursing schools in the midwest, Graceland’s school of nursing offers both in-person and online undergraduate, graduate and doctorate degrees.

Our School of Business offers a wide array of undergraduate majors, dual majors and masters degrees online and at our main residential campus in Lamoni, Iowa.

Deepen your faith with our online Masters of Arts in Religion program that offers three different concentrations in Christian Theology, Spiritual Leadership, Peace and Justice.

In addition to the traditional undergraduate program on our Lamoni campus, we also offer an adult degree completion option on our Independence, MO campus.

### Graceland University Professional Licensure Disclosure

Pursuant to U.S. Federal Regulations34 CFR 668.14(b)(32), Graceland University is required to disclose consumer information regarding curricular programs that “foreseeably lead” to careers requiring state licensure. This regulation requires Graceland University to determine whether or not licensure-related program meets the educational requirements for professional licensure in each U.S. state or territory.

This policy is in accordance with U.S. Federal Regulations amendment34 CFR 668.43(a)(5)(v) & (c)effective as of July 1, 2024. This policy also meets the National Council for State Authorization Reciprocity Agreement (NC-SARA) rules listed in the most recent handbook.

Graceland University academic programs have been designed to meet the licensure/certification in specific disciplines. Since licensure and/or certification requirements vary by jurisdiction and are subject to change,a student considering a program that leads to licensure and/or certification should contact the licensing agency for the state or territory in which they intend to practiceto confirm the most current requirements.

Graceland University makes no representation, warranty, or guarantee that successful completion of the degree or certificate program will permit or guarantee that a state board or licensing agency will accept a graduate’s application for licensure and/or certification. No statement made at any time by any member of the Graceland University faculty or any other representative of Graceland University shall be deemed to be such a representation, warranty, or commitment.Graceland is a participant institution inNC-SARA.
