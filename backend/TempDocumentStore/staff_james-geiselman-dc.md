# James Geiselman, DC, DACBN, CCSP, ICSC, CNC, CES, NREMT

Associate Professor of Allied Health

BA, Psychology, Illinois CollegeBA, Human Biology, Logan UniversityMS, Nutrition and Human Performance Concentration: Sport Fitness Nutrition, Logan UniversityDC, Chiropractic, Logan UniversityCCSP, Certified Chiropractic Sports Physician, Northwestern Health Sciences University

Zimmermann 311

Dr. Geiselman is an Associate Professor of Allied Health at Graceland University in Lamoni, IA. He is also the co-owner of the Iowa Performance Institute where he works as a chiropractic physician focusing on functional health and nutrition. He began his career in healthcare as a paramedic before continuing his education and earning his Doctor of Chiropractic degree in 2015 and graduating Summa Cum Laude with his Master of Science in Nutrition and Human Performance degree in 2017.

At Graceland University, Dr. Geiselman teaches courses within the Division of Health and Movement Science, particularly within the Allied Health major. He helps to prepare students interested in pursuing careers in Physical Therapy, Chiropractic, Athletic Training, Teaching, Coaching, and Strength and Conditioning, to name a few.

Dr. Geiselman has postgraduate training and certifications in Nutrition and Sports Medicine and enjoys volunteering his time with student-athletes at Graceland. He’s earned his Diplomate from the American Clinical Board of Nutrition (DACBN), is a Certified Chiropractic Sports Physician (CCSP), and holds his International Certificate in Sports Chiropractic (ICSC).

He serves on numerous university committees and national organizations. He is the GU Faculty Athletic Representative to the NAIA Heart of America Athletic Conference, Heart Awards Committee, Graceland University Men’s Wrestling Faculty Mentor, Secretary / Treasurer for the American Chiropractic Association’s Council on Nutrition, Associate Editor for the journal Nutritional Foundations, and is the co-host of the Nutrition on a Mission podcast.
