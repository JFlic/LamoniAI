The Performing Arts major at Graceland University is a unique program that offers three distinct tracks: Business and Entrepreneurship, Dramatic Media, and Performance Studies. These tracks allow you focus on the part of the fine arts that most interests you whether it is as a performer, director, or as a key member of the production or marketing team.

Through practical experience in campus productions, you will build a broad resume as a performer, technician, designer, or manager. With a best in southern Iowa recital hall, an expansive auditorium stage and a cutting-edge transformable black box theatre, the Shaw Center offers the perfect backdrop for you to discover your own artistic voice and vision.

Centered around Graceland’s diverse and passionate creative community, the Performing Arts program offers various customization options and scholarship opportunities.All Performing Arts majors complete a broad core of courses followed by one of three distinct tracks.

Centered around Graceland’s diverse and passionate creative community, the Performing Arts program offers various customization options and scholarship opportunities.

All Performing Arts majors complete a broad core of courses followed by one of three distinct tracks.

A hybrid of theatre, film, music, and new media – this track specifically targets the contemporary trends and growth areas in the performing arts. You will receive transformative training in aspects of design and music technology, film, directing, media history, and leadership for a digital performing arts world.

Focused on the business, management, and entrepreneurial side of the performing arts, this concentration gives you the opportunity to learn business practices and apply management skills on campus as a part of the music, production, stage, and box office teams. This is an area that, according to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, is growing nationally. This track is ideally suited for a career as a producer, talent agent, artistic director, executive director, and more.

This track features a focused study and training in music or theatre performance. From all aspects of live and filmed performance, this concentration gives you a holistic approach to what it means to be a performer, with a deep dive into instrumental and vocal performance technique, performance for the stage and screen, immersive script and production analysis, and studies in performance innovation. This is also the track you will take if you are interested in teaching K-12 music or theatre.
